Colorblind Analyzer

Colorblind Analyzer is an internal tool designed for web developers and UX/UI designers to help them create accessible content for users with different types of color vision deficiencies.

Our tool simulates how websites appear to individuals with common types of color blindness, detects potential accessibility issues, and helps teams build inclusive digital experiences. By proactively addressing these issues, teams can meet accessibility standards and deliver a better user experience for everyone.

Why it matters

  • Approximately 1 in 12 men and 1 in 200 women are color blind.
  • Colorblind users may not distinguish red/green buttons or error messages.
  • Designing for accessibility improves usability for all users, not just those with disabilities.

Adobe & Accessibility

  • Adobe has prioritized accessibility across its product suite, including Adobe XD and Acrobat.
  • Adobe offers tools for simulating color blindness in design workflows.
  • Adobe’s accessibility efforts help designers meet WCAG standards and create inclusive experiences.
